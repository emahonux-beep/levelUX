Who's Who

schedule 3 min read

Here's a general map of the roles you'll work with most. Titles and scope vary by team, so treat this as a starting point and confirm specifics with your manager.

Product Manager (PM)

Owns the "what" and "why" — business goals, prioritization, and roadmap. Your main partner for understanding constraints and trade-offs. Loop them in early; they hate surprises at a launch review more than almost anything else.

Engineering Lead / Tech Lead

Owns technical feasibility and implementation. Bring them in during design, not just at handoff — they'll often spot a simpler (or impossible) path before you've sunk hours into a direction.

UX Researcher

Partners with you on understanding user needs. Good researchers will push back on assumptions — treat that as a gift, not an obstacle.

Content Designer / UX Writer

Owns the words: microcopy, error states, tone, terminology. Bring them in while you're still exploring layout — content often reshapes structure.

Design Manager / Lead

Your first stop for craft feedback, career conversations, and unblocking friction. Use 1:1s to flag problems before they become escalations.

Stakeholders / Leadership

Directors, VPs, or cross-functional leads who approve direction or budget. They care about outcomes and risk — frame your work in those terms, not pixels.
