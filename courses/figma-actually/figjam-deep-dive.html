FigJam Deep Dive

schedule 6 min read
🎬 On-screen: title card, "FigJam Deep Dive"

If Design is where you build the thing, FigJam is where you figure out what the thing should be. It's your whiteboard, your workshop room, and your scratch pad for research synthesis — all in one file type. Let's get into what's actually on that toolbar and how to use it well.

The bottom toolbar, tool by tool

🎬 On-screen: screen recording, hovering each tool as it's named
ToolHow much you'll use it
SelectConstantly. This is your default.
HandOccasionally, for navigating a big board.
MarkerRarely. It's genuinely hard to write with a trackpad — typing is almost always faster and more legible.
Sticky notesConstantly. One of your best friends in this tool.
Shapes & connectorsFrequently — this is how you build diagrams like user flows or process maps.
TextFrequently.
SectionsFrequently, and underrated. Sections are how you keep a board from turning into chaos.
TablesOccasionally, including importing straight from Excel — genuinely handy when you've got structured data to bring in.
StampsFor voting, reacting, or flagging something you like during a session.
CommentsConstantly, especially in async or review contexts.

Sections aren't decoration. They're the difference between a board someone can navigate in ten seconds and one they have to scroll around guessing at.

Templates, widgets, and plugins

Figma ships with real templates and widgets built for FigJam specifically — icebreakers, polls, basic diagram starters, workshop activities. Before you build something from scratch, check whether it already exists as a template. There's also a wider plugin ecosystem worth exploring once you're comfortable with the basics.

Running a live session

🎬 On-screen: demo of starting an open session — cursor chat, music, a live vote

FigJam supports real-time collaboration in a way that feels closer to Miro or Mural than to a typical design file. A few things worth knowing:

  • You can open a session and let people move around the board live, which is where the sticky notes and stamps earn their keep.
  • You can run a live vote to get to consensus fast, instead of collecting opinions one Slack thread at a time.
  • You can play music during an open session — small thing, but it changes the energy of a workshop more than you'd expect.

Where FigJam earns its keep

FigJam is genuinely good for:

  • Whiteboarding and low-fidelity exploration
  • Facilitating workshops
  • Collaborating with partners in real time
  • Mapping out ideas — user flows, process flows, journey maps
  • Research synthesis and lightweight data analysis
  • Taking notes during a working session

One underused move: you can copy elements straight from a Design file onto a FigJam board. That's a fast way to bring a real screen into a workshop discussion instead of describing it from memory.
