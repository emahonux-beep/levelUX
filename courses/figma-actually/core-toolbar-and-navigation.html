Module 2 · Craft Fundamentals · Lesson 4 of 10

Core Toolbar & Navigation

schedule 4 min read
🎬 On-screen: title card, "Core Toolbar & Navigation"

Before we get into components and variables, you need to be fast in the interface itself. This lesson is about the tools you'll use every single day and the panels that get you where you need to go without hunting.

The tools that carry most of the work

🎬 On-screen: hover each tool in the toolbar as it's named
ToolWhat it does
MoveSelecting and repositioning anything on the canvas. Your default state.
FrameCreates the containers everything else lives in.
ShapeRectangles, ellipses, lines — the building blocks of most UI.
TextAdding and editing copy directly on the canvas.
CommentLeaving feedback pinned to a specific spot, not floating in a Slack thread.
Dev mode
A dedicated view for handing designs off to engineering — measurements, exportable code snippets, and a place to leave annotations directly on the spec.

There are a few other tools tucked into the toolbar, but these six are where you'll spend nearly all your time. Learning the keyboard shortcuts for each one is genuinely worth the hour it takes — it's the single fastest thing you can do to speed up your day-to-day work.

The left-hand panels

🎬 On-screen: left sidebar, clicking through Pages → Layers → Assets → Tools

Your file navigation lives on the left, and it's structured in layers of its own:

  • Pages — switch between the pages we talked about in Lesson 3.
  • Layers — drill down, click by click, until you reach the exact layer you're trying to edit. On a complex frame this can mean several clicks deep — that's normal, and it's exactly why naming your layers well in Lesson 3 pays off here.
  • Assets — your component libraries live here. This is where a design system actually becomes usable day to day.
  • Tools — plugins, widgets, and other utilities, including things like a CSV-to-variables plugin if you're working with a lot of variable data at once.

Canvas agents

One of the newer additions: you can now use AI agents directly on the canvas instead of jumping out to a separate tool like Figma Make or an external AI product. It's worth knowing this exists even if you don't reach for it on day one — we'll go deeper on this in the AI lesson later in the course.

What's next

Lesson 5 is about working efficiently once you're actually inside a page — sectioning, naming conventions, paste-to-replace, find and replace, and a handful of shortcuts that quietly save you hours over a project.
