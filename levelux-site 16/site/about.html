Why I started this

Learning shouldn't stop when the meeting ends.

I've always believed the best way to actually learn something is to walk through it together. Talk it out live, ask the dumb question, or get something wrong in real time. But live sessions have a problem: they disappear. The slides get filed away, the insights fade, and anyone who missed it is just out of luck.

LevelUX started as a way for me to host a session every once in a blue moon. A reason to get an hour on the calendar for one topic, nothing more. Over time it grew into something I wanted to make permanent. A place where the learning sticks around: for the people who were there, for the ones who weren't, and for me to keep thinking out loud.

I'm a Senior UX Designer who cares a lot about craft, accessibility, and helping other designers grow. This is my way of putting that into practice beyond my day job.
